Writing assignment grades

Writing assignments will be scored with a maximum of 40 points. To convert your grade from this raw score to a 4-point scale, simply move the decimal point one place to the left (or, equivalently, divide by 10). So, for example, a grade of 27/40 becomes 2.7 out of 4.0, which ‚Äî referring to the table above ‚Äî is equivalent to a B‚Äì grade on the A‚ÄìF letter-scale. Test grades

Each answer on tests is scored on a 4-point scale. The overall test grade is then all the answers' scores added together. For example, if there were six test questions, then the highest possible grade would be 24 (= 6 questions x 4 possible points on each).

Your overall score will normally appear on the inside back cover of your test’s blue book. To calculate your overall test grade on a 4-point scale, divide your overall score by the number of questions. For example, if you got a 15/24 on a 6-question test, your 4-point grade would be 15 divided by 6, which is 2.5. On the 4-point scale (see above), 2.5 is equivalent to a C+.

Accordingly, on this system, your overall grade on the test is just the average of the scores you earned on each question.
