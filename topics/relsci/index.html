Topic 3: Religion and Science

Our questions about the meaning of life led to its relationships to two main human institutions from which people seek understanding — religion (especially theology) and science. In this section, we consider the relationship between questions about the meaning of life and the capacities of these institutions.

Reading

  1. Brown, Delwin. 1971. “Process Philosophy and the Question of Life’s Meaning Religious Studies 7(1): 13-29, 10 pages of reading. Access through Axinn Library login here.
  2. Plato, Euthyphro, excerpt. Access via Course Documents on Blackboard.
  3. Hume, David. “The Origin of Ideas,” Section 2 of Enquiry concerning Human Understanding; access here via Early Modern Texts Online.
