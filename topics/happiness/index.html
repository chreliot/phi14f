Topic 5: Happiness

From Epicureans to the present, some philosophers have thought that the meaning of life might lie in seeking happiness. We consider a classic expression of this view in anglophone philosophy, and then some criticisms of it.

Reading

  1. John Stuart Mill, from Utilitarianism, 1861. Read, in the following order:
    • the first section of Chapter 2 “What Utilitarianism Is”, from pages 4 into 5;
    • Chapter 4 “What sort of proof can be given for the principle of utility?” We will also discuss some of the rest of Chapter 2 in class, including looking at the text, and you may find but it is not required reading for class.
  2. Robert Nozick, “The Experience Machine”, an excerpt from Anarchy, State, and Utopia, 1974.
  3. Joel Feinberg, “The Paradox of Hedonism,” via Blackboard, under Course Documents.

See also

  • David Konstan, “Epicurus” in the SEP
  • Catherine Wilson, “How to be an Epicurean” in Aeon magazine, 2019
