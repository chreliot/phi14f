Topic 7: Death

What is the relationship between the end of life and the meaning of life? Surely, if we could live longer with our faculties intact, we could do more. But would our lives be more meaningful if they never ended? Would they be less meaningful?

Reading

  1. Jonathan Swift, Gulliver’s Travels into Several Remote Nations of the World, 1726. Read Part III, Chapter X. Direct link to part 3; scroll down to 10, here.
  2. Bernard Williams, “The Makropulos Case: Reflections on the Tedium of Immortality”, 1973.

See also

  • Jorge Luis Borges, “The Immortal” (a short story)
  • “Death” in the Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, by Steven Luper
  • “The Definition of Death” in the Stanford Encyclopedia of Philosophy, by David DiGrazia
