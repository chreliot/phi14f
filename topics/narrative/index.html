Topic 6: Narrative

Here we explore an account of the meaning of life that takes the idea of “meaning” more seriously than do the others we have discussed. It connects the meaning of life to the the idea of meaning of stories.

Reading

  1. Joshua Seachris, “The Meaning of Life as Narrative: A New Proposal for Interpreting Philosophy’s ‘Primary’ Question” Philo 12:5–23. via Course Documents on Blackboard. This presents a positive account of what could count as an “existentially salient” narrative, such as might constitute the meaning of one’s life.

See also

  1. Oliver Sacks, “The Lost Mariner” in The New York Review of Books, via Hofstra Library proxy. We considered this story in class as the basis for a possible objection to the Narrative Account.
