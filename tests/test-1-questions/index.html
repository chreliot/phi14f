Test 1 Questions

Here are the questions for the first test. The test will be from a subset of these questions. For each question, write at least five sentences (minimum) and perhaps a bit more than five. Write concisely and informatively. If there is more than one part to the question, make sure to answer all the parts.

  1. What is Blackburn’s “middle-ground” defense of philosophy.
  2. What is Blackburn’s “low-ground” defense of philosophy.
  3. In Apology, what does Socrates say is the greatest good for a human being (“a man”), and what does he say that supports that? (That is, why, according to Socrates, might that the greatest good for a human being?)
  4. In Gorgias, Socrates distinguishes between two forms of persuasion. How, according to him, does this help show why learning philosophy is better than learning what Gorgias teaches?
  5. In Gorgias, Socrates suggests that power isn’t always good. Why?
  6. In Gorgias, Socrates suggests that fulfilling our appetites isn’t always good. Why?
  7. What are three different ways the existence of God might be relevant to the meaning of our lives?
  8. What, in your view, is the most questionable premise in Anselm’s “First Way” argument. How might Anselm try to talk about it being true?
  9. How does a reductio ad absurdum argument work, and what is the absurdity Gaunilo uses in his rebuttal to Anselm’s proof of the existence of God?
  10. Does Pascal’s argument conclude that God exists? What does it conclude? What, in your view, is successful or promising about the way it argues?
  11. What is the error Wallace suggests most people make in our thinking? How, according to him, might freeing ourselves of that error make our lives better?
  12. What is the main assumption about the nature of reality outside us that stands behind Epictetus’s thinking about how we should live?
  13. How might Epictetus respond to the suggestion that a life filled with suffering can’t be a good life?
  14. What is one criticism of the Stoic account of how to live the best possible life?
  15. What idea about the meaning of life, from all of what we’ve discussed so far, did you connect with most? Why did it seem compelling to you?
